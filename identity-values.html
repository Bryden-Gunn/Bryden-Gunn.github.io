Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.


Moving away from the Avatar Community facing my destructive financial habits and paying off my debts.

Form the age of 14-23 My family and I were involved in a highly manipulative and controlling self development community called “The Avatar Course”. Very similar to Scientology in which people are enticed to sign up for 2-9 day seminars in which confidential materials are delivered to students as a course. Students are guided through some very powerful rundowns which often produce some extremely compelling experiences. Being that you are going through these experiences together you form a close bond with the group and an understanding between you that is unlike the typical relationships in our lives. Unfortunately despite the inherent value in some of the material the overall culture of the group was centred around bringing on more recruits and growing the organisation. We were made to feel as though it was imperative that we have more and more people go through these courses for the sake of the future of human-kind.

The group would often place ones “spiritual evolution” above peoples financial well-being and over all mental health. Therefore people were often encouraged to take out bank loans or borrow money from wealthier members of the community in order to make it to these workshops. Taking the time to save up for the trip and going at a later date was always discouraged. Subsequently, like many others, I found myself in quite a bit of personal debt with no real means of paying it all back. Let alone at 23 I had no tangible tertiary education hadn’t done any “OE” like many of my peers and was very mentally unstable. I knew that I needed to move on from this group, find myself a job, pay back all my debts and make a life for myself that was worth living on my own terms.

Despite also having a strong passion for music and wanting to get a degree in Jazz I knew that if I didn’t pay this debt off I would be in debt for most of my 20s and would have missed out on the basic joys of life as a young adult. So I found myself a customer service role at a bank and grinded my way to freedom. In total I owed approx. $13,000.00 and within 18months I had paid it all off.

As a result I am now free to do something such as dev academy in which I can take time to earn a qualification and find a full-filling Vocation. Not to mention one day afford to travel as well as support my creative outlets.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

Being part of the Avatar network wasn’t all bad. One way in which that culture has influenced me is that I very rarely find myself in conflict with others, I am always willing to look at my own actions and first consider where I am at fault before criticising others.

I have been lucky to meet some very skilled professional musicians. Being part of a music scene meant that I adopted a lot of their values. The main one being that Endeavouring to reach your full potential within your creative outlet is what is most fulfilling in life. That if you are creative then that part of you will never die and neglecting it will only bring you sadness. Honouring it and letting it out will bring you joy like nothing else can.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.


Making a song a day

Recently a friend and I got together and set ourselves the challenge of writing and recording a song per day over the period of four days. One common problem that would come up was differences between us in beliefs about how music should be made. I would often want to record everything from scratch and focus more heavily on music theory. Where as, my friend was more prepared to use pre-made loops and focus more on the audio production side of things. A strategy we used at the time was that if we couldn’t do it quickly enough to finish the song in a day then we were over thinking it and needed to simplify things. You could say we adopted a “Not yet” approach when we were concerned about our level of musicianship.

Looking back on it I would lean even further toward simplifying my ideas for songs. Great Pop songs are always very very simple when it comes to whats going on musically but just because it’s simple doesn’t mean its stupid. I would be less emotionally attached to the outcome and treat the experience as a process of discovery as apposed to searching for a particular outcome.
