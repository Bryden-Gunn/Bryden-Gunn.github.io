how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Life is inherently unfair. No matter who you are, you have probably had experiences in your childhood or early adulthood that messed with your sense of self esteem, your sense of safety, you’re sense of belonging in your community and your sense of capability to take on new challenges and reap the rewards of hard work. Even if from the outside your life could have looked ideal there could always be those silent solitary and unconventional struggles that plague your experience of being human.

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity offers you the reassurance that no matter what situation you were born into in this world, you can slowly inch your way toward the situation that you hoped for. As long as you are prepared to fully own your past and the ways in which it disadvantaged you the burden of it can, bit by bit, be lifted from you and you can create an experience of life for yourself that is truly your own

How I might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for my benefit

I will be engaging in the principles of neuroplasticity for the benefit of both my professional and personal life. In relation to learning to program I will make a habit of attempting problems/projects that are just beyond my capability. Aiming to continually push the limit of my skills by attempting problems that stretch me will keep me in the zone of proximal development. Staying in this zone on a regular basis will no doubt increase my ability to continue learning throughout my career. For instance: once EDA is finished, set a goal of doing ten additional projects. Each involving a new technology that I will likely be using in the type of junior role I hope to land.

As far as my personal life goes I hope to keep improving my social skills by getting out of my comfort zone and going to all sorts of events/meetups and interacting with as many people as I can. I am considering moving to a new city once I graduate. Generating meaningful new connections and turning interesting strangers into new friends does absolute wonders for my self esteem and sense of hope for my future. I plan to prove to myself repeatedly in small ways over time that I am worthy of having a tight friend group, a great relationship and can make a meaningful contribution to my community.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Engage in more effort based reward activities. Type two fun you could say. Strenuous exercise, reading books, in person group activities, working with your hands. Activities that put effort before reward. As apposed to, excessive drinking, excessive smart phone use, unhealthy food, staying sedentary in your room all day and not leaving the house. It could be boiled down to the simple idea of taking the path of most resistance. Doing all the things that you do not want to do, but you really know you should.

Growth mindset

Having a Growth mindset is about having an attitude that leans toward challenging experiences that help you grow. Reducing the need for validation of ones existing skills and instead being prepared to double down on ones weaknesses in order to improve. The less you are caught up in the idea of trying to prove that your gifted or special in some way and accepting that you are more or less like everyone else. That everyone has the ability to learn but it’s the ones that put in the hard work that ultimately end up doing something meaningful with their skills. It’s about choosing to be excited by new challenges and having faith that as long as you take the time to understand, demystify, break down problems into smaller parts and set realistic short term goals that you will eventually get it and become competent. It’s important because it’s the difference between reaching your full potential or living a life so comfortable that you never got to find out what you were capable of.

I will be incorporating the core ideas of make it stick into my coding practice. Self quizzing, interleaving and spacing effect. What I hope to do is increase my memory recall and become less and less reliant on the previous solutions people have found on stack overflow. I hope to be more original and genuinely come up with my own solutions to problems.

I will also embrace the ideas of Range. By this I mean embrace the fact that I have tried so many things throughout my life and use that to my advantage. I will use the concepts that I have learned while learning a musical instrument, working in a woodworking shop, working in a bank, studying film production and apply them to programming. This is something I was anxious about in regards to finding a job after dev academy, but now I know how I can present my previous work and life experience as evidence that I have “range” and that that is what will make me valuable to a company in the long run.
